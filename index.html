Overview: A. Jude Robinson Fellowship

The A. Jude Robinson fellowship is world renouned in the field of vascular surgery. High calibre fellows rise to the challenge of applying cutting edge reasearch during their fellowship. Fellows are expected to take on leadership of their specialist vascular field. Our graduating fellows form the world's elite vascular surgeons. They are the authority in their specialisation and are free to practice without supervision.

History: A. Jude Robinson

A. Jude Robinson

A. Jude Robinson was an eminent scientist from California who made a huge mark on the field of vascular surgery when he pioneered the Robinson technique for the first time in 1969.
Robinson was born on 24 January 1923. He was exposed to the horrors of war, serving in the 3rd brigade who landed on Omaha beach. He went to live and study at Havard University after the war, under the tutilage of Dr. Kier Wilson. Robinson served his residency at New Yoork Hospital beforre completing a stint as fellow in Boston.
Chicago soon beckoned for Robinson. At Chicago Memorial Hospital, he pushed the boundaries of what was possible with his innovative research methods. The pinnicle was the Robinson technique. For this, he received a MDA Medal of distinction.

Controversy

The Nichols Scandal was a black time for the fellowship. Nichols was accused of falsifying his research in order to gain a directorship at Devlin McGregor. Evidence was damaged in a freak fire. Nichols spent 4 years in Chicago penetentary. He re-entered medicine in Russia.
